Some of the club's idols

Martin Palermo

Martin Palermo, 2000.

Martin Palermo is a former player, world class striker, that saw his best shape playing for Club Atletico Boca Juniors between 1997-2011, being the Club's all time top scorer with 236 goals.

Juan Roman Riquelme

Juan Roman Riquelme, after winning the 2007 "Copa Libertadores".

Juan Roman Riquelme is Boca's actual vice President, but he is also one of the club's most historical players, winning not only 3 "Copas Libertadores", but also winning an intercontinental against non others than Real Madrid. Riquelme also had a brief spell in Europe, but saw his best shape in his backyard, "La Bombonera".

Sebastian Battaglia

Sebastian Battaglia, 2004.

Sebastian Battaglia is a former player and manager of Boca Juniors. Battaglia is the most decorated player in Boca Juniors' history, having won 17 titles with the club between 1998-2012. His last championship playing for boca was the 2011-12 Copa Argentina.

Carlos Bianchi

Carlos "El Virrey" Bianchi, 1994.

Carlos Bianchi, known as "El Virrey", is one of the most influential managers of Argentinian and south American football. He had to successfull spells as Boca's manager, 1998-2001 and 2003-2004, where the club picked up 9 trophies; four local tournaments (1998/1999/2000/2003) and five internationals (copa Libertadores 2000-2001-2003 and two Intercontinental Cups 2000 & 2003).

Come here a funny one...

Palermo is also known for missing 3 penalties in a single game with Argentina's National team. The 4 of July of 1999 Argentina was playing against Colombia, and the striker had 3 opportunities to score from the penalty kick, but he managed to missed all three of them, granting him a special place in football's history books. After that day, he didn't play for Argentina's National team for almost a decade, coming back in 2009 to give his team the qualification for the following World Cup of 2010, where he would debut in the world cup and score a goal against Greece.

Honorable Mentions

Diego Armando Maradona

Diego Armando Maradona had a brief stay in La Boca before departing to Europe in 1981. He would then come back to his beloved club in 1995 to 1998, where he would retire officially from the sport.

Carlos Tevez

Carlos Tevez is a player formed in Boca Juniors' academy, one of the clubs finest talents. With the club he won everything he could, from "Copa Libertadores", "Intercontinental Cup 2003", and every local title there is to win. "El Apache", known like this because he was born in one of Argentina's most dangerous neighbourhoods "Fuerte Apache", came back to Argentina after 10 years in Europe to spend the last years of his career in the Club that saw him grow.

Hugo Ibarra

Hugo Benjamin Ibarra, also known as "El Negro", is a former right back (defender) from Argentina. He is one of the players with the most titles won for the club, right after Battaglia. He has won 5 "Copas Libertadores" and a "Intercontinental Cup".
